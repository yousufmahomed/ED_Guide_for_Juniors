NSTEMI / High-Risk ACS – ED Checklist

NSTEMI / High-Risk ACS

Dynamic troponin • Ischaemic ECG • Ongoing pain

Diagnostic Features

Yes No Feature
Typical or atypical chest pain compatible with ACS
Ischaemic ECG changes (ST depression / T wave inversion)
Dynamic troponin rise or fall above 99th percentile
Haemodynamic instability or arrhythmia
Diabetes, CKD, prior MI, known CAD

NSTEMI = non-ST elevation MI (troponin positive) with ischaemic symptoms/ECG.

Risk Stratification (Conceptual)

  • Very high risk (needs urgent angiography):
    • Ongoing chest pain despite treatment
    • Haemodynamic instability, shock, or life-threatening arrhythmia
    • Dynamic ST changes (widespread ST depression with aVR STE)
  • High risk: elevated troponin + ischaemic ECG changes, diabetes, prior MI/CABG, reduced EF.
  • Low–Intermediate risk: non-diagnostic ECG, borderline troponin, no red flags.

ED Management

  1. Initial measures
    • Monitor, IV access, ECG, bloods, CXR (if indicated).
    • Aspirin 300 mg chewed (if not already given).
  2. Anti-ischaemic therapy
    • GTN SL/IV if pain + hypertension, provided no RV infarct / hypotension.
    • Beta-blocker if hypertensive/tachycardic and no contraindications.
  3. Antiplatelet therapy
    • Add second antiplatelet (clopidogrel/ticagrelor) as per local protocol & cardiology advice.
  4. Anticoagulation
    • LMWH or fondaparinux/heparin per NSTEMI protocol.
  5. Analgesia & symptom control
  6. Decide disposition: CCU / high care / monitored ward vs short-stay with serial biomarkers and stress testing.

Red Flags – Treat as Very High Risk ACS

  • Ongoing chest pain despite GTN and analgesia
  • Haemodynamic instability (SBP < 90, signs of shock)
  • Recurrent VT/VF, high-degree AV block
  • Dynamic ST depression / transient ST elevation
  • Acute heart failure / pulmonary oedema

These patients often need urgent angiography (within hours), not days.
