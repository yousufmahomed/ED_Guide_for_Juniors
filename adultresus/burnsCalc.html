Burn Fluid Replacement Calculator

Burn Fluid Replacement Calculator

Uses the Parkland formula: 4 mL × weight (kg) × %TBSA over the first 24 hours from the time of burn. This tool is for major burns – always adjust to the clinical picture and urine output.

Patient Weight

Percentage Burn (TBSA)

Parkland resuscitation usually applies to ≥15–20% TBSA in adults (≥10% in children). Smaller burns often need analgesia, dressings, and maintenance fluids only.

Time Since Burn

The first 8 hours are counted from the time of burn, not from ED arrival. Leave blank or 0 if unsure.

Pre-hospital Fluids (Optional)

If you know how much fluid has already been given, enter it here to adjust the remaining volume.

Important: This calculator estimates initial fluid volumes only. Always titrate to clinical response and urine output (e.g. ~0.5 mL/kg/h in adults). In children, add maintenance fluids separately and follow paediatric burn protocols.
