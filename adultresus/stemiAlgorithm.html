STEMI – ED Algorithm

Door-to-ECG < 10 min • Door-to-Balloon < 90 min

1. Triage & First 10 Minutes

  • Any chest pain / dyspnoea / syncope → treat as possible ACS.
  • Attach monitor, BP, SpO₂, IV line, 12-lead ECG within 10 minutes.
  • Take focused history (onset, character, risk factors, meds).

Do not delay ECG for bloods, cannula, or X-ray.

⬇

2. ECG Interpretation – Is this STEMI?

  • ST elevation in 2+ contiguous leads meeting STEMI criteria OR
  • New/presumed new LBBB with typical symptoms OR
  • STEMI equivalents:
    • Posterior MI: ST depression V1–V3 → do V7–V9
    • De Winter T-waves, Wellens, aVR STE pattern

If yes → activate STEMI pathway.

⬇

3. Immediate ED Treatment (Do Now)

  • Aspirin 300 mg chewed (if not already given).
  • Consider P2Y12 loading (clopidogrel / ticagrelor) as per local protocol.
  • Oxygen only if SpO₂ < 90% or in respiratory distress.
  • GTN SL if SBP ≥ 90 and no RV infarct or recent PDE-5 inhibitor.
  • Analgesia (low-dose morphine IV) if pain severe.
  • Take bloods (troponin, FBC, U&E, coagulation, glucose).
⬇

4. Decide Reperfusion Strategy

  • Primary PCI preferred if:
    • PCI available and can be done within 120 min of first medical contact.
  • Thrombolysis if:
    • PCI delay > 120 min AND
    • No absolute contraindications to lysis AND
    • Within 12 hours of symptom onset (best < 3 hours).

Call cardiology / STEMI line early – let them say no.

⬇

5A. If Primary PCI Chosen

  • Give DAPT + anticoagulation per local protocol.
  • Arrange immediate transfer to cath lab.
  • Continue monitoring: rhythm, BP, pain, complications.

Door-to-balloon target: < 90 minutes.

⬇

5B. If Thrombolysis Chosen

  • Check absolute and relative contraindications carefully.
  • Administer thrombolytic (e.g. tenecteplase/alteplase) per protocol.
  • Give anticoagulation (e.g. enoxaparin/heparin) per protocol.
  • Monitor for:
    • Resolution of pain and ST elevation (≥ 50% in lead with max STE).
    • Bleeding, stroke, arrhythmias, hypotension.
  • If failed lysis → plan rescue PCI.
⬇

6. Ongoing Care & Disposition

  • Admit to CCU/ICU where possible.
  • Start secondary prevention once stable:
    • Beta-blocker, ACEi/ARB, statin, DAPT, +/- MRA.
  • Assess LV function, complications (MR, VSD, HF, arrhythmias).

Document times: symptom onset, first medical contact, ECG, needle/balloon.
