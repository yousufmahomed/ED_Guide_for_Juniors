Non-shockable Cardiac Arrest Management in the ED

Step 1: Assess and Confirm Cardiac Arrest

  • Check responsiveness
  • Check for pulse and breathing
  • Call for the Code Blue or Rapid Response Team if needed

Step 2: Initiate CPR and Airway Management

  • Start chest compressions
  • Perform bag-valve-mask (BVM) ventilation or advanced airway management (e.g., intubation) as appropriate
  • Continue CPR, and coordinate compressions with ventilations

Step 3: Intravenous (IV) or Intraosseous (IO) Access and Drug Administration

  • Establish IV or IO access
  • Administer appropriate medications (e.g., epinephrine, vasopressin)
  • Consider reversible causes and treat accordingly (e.g., naloxone for opioid overdose)

Step 4: Monitor and Manage Reversible Causes

  • Assess for and address reversible causes (e.g., hypoxia, hypovolemia, tension pneumothorax)
  • Obtain and interpret bedside tests (e.g., arterial blood gas, point-of-care ultrasound)
  • Consider consultation with cardiology or critical care specialists

Step 5: Post-resuscitation Care

  • Stabilize and optimize the patient's condition
  • Arrange for transfer to the appropriate unit (e.g., ICU)
  • Consider targeted temperature management
