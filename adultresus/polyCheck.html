Polytrauma Checklist

YES NO Reccomendations/ Suggestions
Multiple injuries in one or more anatomical regions (excluding isolated limb injuries)
Injury severity score (ISS) > 15
Head injury (Glasgow Coma Scale < 13)
Spinal cord injury (motor or sensory deficit)
Chest injury (flail chest, tension pneumothorax, hemothorax, or significant pulmonary contusion)
Abdominal injury (intra-abdominal hemorrhage or organ injury)
Pelvic injury (open or unstable fractures, diastasis of pubic symphysis or sacroiliac joint)
Extremity injury with ischemia or compartment syndrome
History of anticoagulation or antiplatelet therapy Consider reversal of anticoagulation or antiplatelet therapy if needed
Suspected or confirmed pregnancy Consider fetal monitoring and obstetric consultation

Initial Management

  • Primary survey and resuscitation as per Advanced Trauma Life Support (ATLS) protocol
  • Control external bleeding and hemorrhage
  • Assess airway, breathing, and circulation (ABCs)
  • Obtain intravenous access and administer crystalloid fluids
  • Consider tranexamic acid for significant bleeding
  • Consider early intubation for airway protection or respiratory distress
  • Consider pelvic stabilization for pelvic fractures
  • Consider imaging studies (CT scan, X-rays) as needed

Secondary Management

  • Consult appropriate specialists (e.g. trauma surgeon, neurosurgeon, orthopedic surgeon)
  • Consider early transfer to a trauma center if available
  • Consider analgesia and sedation as needed
  • Consider prophylaxis for deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and stress ulceration
  • Consider tetanus prophylaxis if indicated

Tertiary Management

  • Arrange for follow-up and rehabilitation
  • Consider psychosocial support for patient and family
