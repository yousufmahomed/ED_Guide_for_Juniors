Crush Syndrome Checklist

YES NO Recommendations/ Suggestions
Compressive force or prolonged immobilization
Pain and swelling of the affected area
Restricted movement of the affected area
Numbness or tingling sensation in the affected area
Dark-colored urine (myoglobinuria)
Rhabdomyolysis (elevated creatine kinase and potassium levels)
Acute kidney injury (elevated BUN and creatinine levels)
Hyperkalemia (high potassium levels)
Hypocalcemia (low calcium levels)
Metabolic acidosis (low blood pH)

Management of Crush Syndrome

  1. Stabilize the patient (airway, breathing, circulation)
  2. Remove the compressive force or release the trapped limb
  3. Administer intravenous fluids (isotonic saline) to prevent kidney injury
  4. Administer pain relief medications (e.g., analgesics, anti-inflammatory drugs)
  5. Administer diuretics if needed, to increase urine output and remove myoglobin
  6. Monitor electrolyte levels and correct any imbalances (e.g., hyperkalemia, hypocalcemia)
  7. Administer bicarbonate for metabolic acidosis if necessary
  8. Regularly monitor vital signs, kidney function, and laboratory tests
  9. Consult a nephrologist for advanced management of acute kidney injury
  10. Consider dialysis if kidney function does not improve or worsens
