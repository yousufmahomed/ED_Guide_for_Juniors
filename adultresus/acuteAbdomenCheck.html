Acute Abdomen Checklist

YES NO Recommendations/ Suggestions
Sudden onset of severe abdominal pain
Localized or generalized tenderness in the abdomen
Rebound tenderness or guarding
Signs of peritonitis (rigid abdomen, severe tenderness)
Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea
Loss of appetite
Fever or chills
Abdominal distension
Signs of dehydration (e.g., dry mucous membranes, decreased urine output)
Altered bowel habits (constipation or diarrhea)

Management of Acute Abdomen

  1. Stabilize the patient (airway, breathing, circulation)
  2. Perform a thorough physical examination, including auscultation of bowel sounds
  3. Obtain a detailed history, including the onset, location, and intensity of pain
  4. Order appropriate laboratory tests (e.g., complete blood count, electrolytes, liver function tests, amylase, lipase)
  5. Consider imaging studies (e.g., X-ray, ultrasound, CT scan) to identify the cause
  6. Administer pain relief medications (e.g., analgesics, anti-inflammatory drugs) cautiously, as they may mask symptoms
  7. Administer intravenous fluids if dehydration is present
  8. Consider nasogastric tube insertion for bowel decompression if needed
  9. Consult a surgeon for further evaluation and potential surgical intervention
  10. Monitor the patient's vital signs and symptoms closely, and adjust management accordingly
