  • Magnesium Sulphate 200 mg in 200ml crystalloid over 30min (At this stage medicine must be involved)

Important Considerations

  1. Don't delay intubation in a patient with multiple danger signs
  2. Intubate with the largest diameter ET Tube you can
  3. These patients decompensate quickly
  4. Repeated nebs will drop your K+ levels
  5. Most of these patients will do better in ICU, decide quickly on admission

Emergency Investigations

  • Bedside CXR: These Patients Must not leave A&E
  • Baseline bloods
  • ABG:
    • Normal to low ph: Indicates Severity
    • Lactate levels rising: Ominous sign
    • PCO2 Levels: If > 9, intubate
