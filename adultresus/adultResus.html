POCUS & E-FAST for Trauma

POCUS and E-FAST are essential for shocked or trauma patients, but they are difficult to learn from text alone. Probe position, patient positioning, and dynamic views are best seen in motion.

For high-quality POCUS teaching, see Dr Johnny Wilkinson’s ultrasound playlist .

We also have our own ED ultrasound playlist (ER Mentorship Hub) , which goes into more depth, although the production quality may be less refined.

Senior tip: in unstable trauma, use E-FAST to answer focused questions quickly – “is there free fluid?”, “is there pneumothorax?” – and act on the result rather than chasing a perfect scan.
