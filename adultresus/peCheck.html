Wells' Score for Pulmonary Embolism

Feature Points
Clinical signs and symptoms of DVT (leg swelling, pain with palpation of the deep veins, or unilateral pitting edema)
Alternative diagnosis less likely than PE
Heart rate ≥ 100 beats/minute
Immobilization or surgery in the previous 4 weeks
History of DVT or PE
Recent hemoptysis (within 1 month)
An active malignant condition (currently receiving treatment or diagnosed within the previous 6 months)

Management

  • If Wells' score is high (≥4), order CT pulmonary angiography (CTPA) or ventilation/perfusion (V/Q) scan
  • If Wells' score is low (<4), consider D-dimer test if no contraindication
  • If CTPA or V/Q scan is negative and low pretest probability of PE, no further imaging is required
  • If D-dimer test is positive, perform CTPA or V/Q scan
  • If D-dimer test is negative, no further testing is required
  • In patients with contraindications to anticoagulation therapy, consider placement of inferior vena cava filter
  • In massive or submassive PE with hemodynamic instability, consider thrombolytic therapy or surgical embolectomy
  • In non-massive PE without hemodynamic instability, start anticoagulation therapy
  • In patients with underlying malignancy, consider placement of inferior vena cava filter and long-term anticoagulation therapy
  • For patients on anticoagulation therapy, monitor for bleeding complications and adjust dosages as necessary
