Myocardial Infarction Checklist

YES NO Recommendations/ Suggestions
Chest pain or discomfort
Pain radiating to arm, neck, jaw, shoulder, back or epigastrium
Shortness of breath or dyspnea
Nausea or vomiting
Sweating or diaphoresis
Anxiety, restlessness, or sense of impending doom
New onset of fatigue or weakness
ECG changes ST-segment elevation, new LBBB, or ST-segment depression

Emergency Interventions

  • Administer aspirin 300mg orally as soon as possible
  • Administer nitroglycerin sublingually or spray (0.4mg) every 5 minutes for up to three doses
  • Administer morphine (2-4mg IV every 5-15 minutes) for pain relief
  • Administer oxygen via nasal cannula or face mask as needed
  • Prepare for transfer to cardiac catheterization lab or administration of thrombolytic therapy

Thrombolytic Contraindications

  • Active bleeding or bleeding diathesis
  • History of hemorrhagic stroke
  • Recent head trauma or intracranial surgery within 3 months
  • Recent major surgery within 3 weeks
  • Known intracranial neoplasm, arteriovenous malformation, or aneurysm
  • Severe uncontrolled hypertension (systolic blood pressure > 180 mm Hg or diastolic blood pressure > 110 mm Hg)
  • Active peptic ulcer disease
  • Current use of anticoagulants or recent use of heparin (within 48 hours) with a prolonged activated partial thromboplastin time (aPTT)

Thrombolytic Relative Contraindications

  • Prior ischemic stroke within 3 months
  • Recent gastrointestinal or genitourinary bleeding within 4 weeks
  • Recent minor trauma or surgery within 2 weeks
  • Pregnancy
  • Recent internal bleeding within 6 months
  • Noncompressible vascular punctures
  • Diabetic hemorrhagic retinopathy or other hemorrhagic ophthalmic conditions
  • Recent cardiopulmonary resuscitation
