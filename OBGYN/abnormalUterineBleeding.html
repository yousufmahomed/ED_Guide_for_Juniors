Abnormal Uterine Bleeding

Introduction

An understanding of the normal menstrual cycle is essential to diagnose and manage abnormal uterine bleeding (AUB). The PALM-COEIN classification system helps categorize the causes of AUB.

Menstrual Cycle and PALM-COEIN Classification

  • Menstrual cycle involves hormonal changes, ovulation, and withdrawal of hormones leading to menstruation.
  • PALM-COEIN classification system categorizes causes of AUB: PALM for structural causes and COEIN for nonstructural causes.

PALM-COEIN Classification System

Category Cause
PALM Structural Causes
P: Polyp
A: Adenomyosis
L: Leiomyoma (fibroids)
M: Malignancy and hyperplasia
COEIN Nonstructural Causes
C: Coagulopathy
O: Ovulatory dysfunction
E: Endometrial
I: Iatrogenic
N: Not yet classified

Clinical Presentation

  • AUB is defined as any change in frequency, regularity, duration, or volume of bleeding.
  • Assess patient history, menstrual cycle pattern, volume and frequency of bleeding, and any associated symptoms.
  • Consider age-related causes of AUB, such as puberty, perimenopause, or postmenopause.

Physical Examination

  • Perform a systematic physical examination focused on signs of hypovolemia, anemia, and underlying conditions.
  • Evaluate for structural lesions, coagulopathy, thyroid dysfunction, and genital tract pathology.

Diagnostic Evaluation

  • Perform a urine or serum pregnancy test in reproductive-age patients.
  • Hemoglobin or hematocrit should be measured in cases of excessive bleeding or anemia suspicion.
  • Screen for coagulopathies, thyroid dysfunction, and sexually transmitted infections.
  • Transvaginal ultrasound (TVUS) may be used to evaluate structural causes, endometrial thickness, and more in-depth assessment of the uterus.

Management

  • Hemodynamically unstable patients with uncontrolled bleeding require standard resuscitation measures and may need surgical interventions.
  • Intrauterine tamponade, estrogen therapy, or medical options like tranexamic acid can be considered for acute management.
  • Oral contraceptives and hormonal IUDs can help regulate the menstrual cycle and treat AUB caused by hormonal imbalance.

Nonhormonal Medications

  • NSAIDs and tranexamic acid can be used to manage pain and bleeding associated with AUB.

Disposition

  • Most patients with AUB can be managed on an outpatient basis, with referral to a gynecologist for further evaluation and management.
  • Severe cases of acute AUB with hemodynamic instability require urgent gynecologic consultation and may necessitate hospitalization.

Patient Counseling

  • Educate patients about the potential causes of AUB, available treatment options, and the importance of follow-up care.

Multidisciplinary Collaboration

Collaborate with gynecologists for timely intervention and management of AUB cases that require further evaluation.
