Primary Survey in Maternal Care

When dealing with maternal emergencies, it is crucial to perform a primary survey to assess and prioritize the mother's health and obtain preliminary information about the fetus's age. Here are the key steps in the maternal primary survey:

Airway and Breathing

  • Institute oxygen therapy early to ensure adequate oxygenation.
  • Maintain oxygen saturation levels above 95%.
  • Consider rapid sequence intubation after pre-oxygenation if necessary.
  • Adjust mechanical ventilation settings as needed.

Circulation

  • Ensure intravenous access with two large-bore catheters placed above the diaphragm.
  • Monitor maternal blood pressure and heart rate.
  • Begin fluid resuscitation with isotonic fluids if significant blood loss is suspected.
  • Use Type O-negative packed red blood cells until type-specific blood products are available.
  • Initiate a massive transfusion protocol with a 1:1:1 ratio of red blood cells, platelets, and plasma.
  • Administer tranexamic acid within 3 hours of injury.
  • Implement a left lateral tilt of 15 to 30 degrees if the gestation is beyond 20 weeks.
  • Use a Foley catheter to measure urine output for circulatory volume status evaluation.

Assessment of Uterine Size and Fetal Viability

  • Measure uterine size from the symphysis pubis to the fundus to estimate gestational age.
  • Check fetal heart tones through auscultation or Doppler probe.

The maternal primary survey is a critical step in maternal care during emergencies, focusing on the mother's well-being while considering the age and viability of the fetus.
