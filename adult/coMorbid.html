Past History and Co-Morbid Conditions

Co-Morbidities

A useful mnemonic is D-E-A-T-H: Diabetes, Epilepsy, Asthma, TB, Hypertension/HIV.

In this section we add a few more important conditions that are worth asking about in the emergency setting.

Diabetes Type I

  • Get a detailed history of medications and dosages (insulin type, units, frequency).
  • Ask about previous episodes of DKA or severe hypoglycaemia.
  • Note any diabetes-related complications (neuropathy, retinopathy, renal disease).

Diabetes Type II

  • Age of onset and current medications (oral agents, insulin, GLP-1, etc.).
  • Dietary habits, body habitus and family history of diabetes.
  • Previous admissions related to diabetes (DKA, HHS, sepsis, foot sepsis, stroke, MI).

Epilepsy

  • Age of onset and seizure type (generalised, focal, absence, myoclonic).
  • Current medications and dosages (and any recent changes).
  • Was the epilepsy spontaneous or secondary to trauma, infection, vascular lesion or tumour?
  • Time since the last seizure and any recent increase in seizure frequency.
  • Medication adherence and any known triggers (sleep deprivation, alcohol, missed doses).

Asthma

  • Regular medications and dosages (SABA, ICS, LABA, oral steroids).
  • Age of onset and known triggers (infections, exercise, allergens, occupational exposures).
  • Previous admissions, especially ICU or intubations for status asthmaticus.
  • Recent steroid use, frequent nebulisations or ED visits in the last year.

TB (Tuberculosis)

  • Important: If possible, examine the TB DOTS (green) card or equivalent.
  • Note duration of treatment, regimen, dosage changes, side effects and complications.
  • If the card is not available, obtain as much detail as possible from the patient or family.
  • Differentiate the type of TB:
    • Pulmonary
    • Abdominal
    • Joint
    • Spinal
    • Meningitis
    • Intracranial TB (tuberculomas, etc.)

Hypertension

  • Document medications and dosages and ask about adherence.
  • Ask about any known secondary causes (renal disease, endocrine causes, etc.).
  • Previous admissions for hypertensive emergencies or strokes.
  • Any known target organ damage: CKD, LVH/heart failure, previous MI, retinopathy, stroke.

HIV

  • Ask if the patient knows their HIV status and when they were last tested.
  • Current ART regimen, prophylaxis (e.g. cotrimoxazole), and adherence.
  • History of opportunistic infections (TB, PCP, cryptococcal meningitis, etc.).
  • Any known CD4 count and viral load (and when they were last checked).
  • Drug allergies or previous adverse reactions to ART.

Other Important Medical Conditions

The following is a concise (but not exhaustive) list of common medical conditions to ask about:

  • Ischaemic Heart Disease (previous MI, stents, CABG).
  • Congestive Cardiac Failure.
  • Valvular Heart Disease / Rheumatic Heart Disease.
  • Known arrhythmias (AF, flutter, VT, long QT, WPW, pacemaker, ICD).
  • COPD and other chronic lung diseases (bronchiectasis, fibrosis).
  • Recurrent Pneumonia or chronic infections.
  • Lung Cancer or other malignancies (with site and treatment history).
  • Renal impairment:
    • History of dialysis (HD, PD) or use of chelating agents or high-dose diuretics.
  • Liver disease:
    • Cirrhosis, liver cancer, cysts and lesions.
    • Gallbladder disease (cholecystitis, stones, previous ERCP).
  • Previous stroke (CVA) or TIA.
  • Space-occupying lesions (brain tumours, abscesses, etc.).
    • Always clarify baseline neurological function (speech, mobility, cognition).
  • Previous ICU or High-Care admissions and the reasons for those admissions.

Other Surgical Diagnoses

A concise (but not exhaustive) list of common surgical conditions to ask about:

  • Previous abdominal surgery (type and indication).
  • Appendicitis and other acute abdominal conditions.
  • Previous traumatic brain injury or neurosurgery.
  • Hyper- or hypothyroidism requiring surgery or radioactive iodine.
  • GIT ulceration or bleeding (e.g. peptic ulcer disease).
  • History of bowel obstruction or adhesions.
  • Hernias (inguinal, femoral, incisional, etc.):
    • Ask about past incarceration, strangulation or repairs.
  • Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) or Pulmonary Embolism (PE).
  • Peripheral vascular disease.
  • Amputations:
    • Clarify the underlying cause (vascular, trauma, diabetes, infection, tumour).
  • Again, note any previous ICU or High-Care admissions related to surgery.

Other Orthopaedic Diagnoses

Common orthopaedic conditions worth asking about:

  • Previous fractures (site, mechanism, complications).
  • ORIF (Open Reduction Internal Fixation) or other orthopaedic implants.
  • Amputations (level and reason).
  • Ligament or tendon injuries (ACL, rotator cuff, Achilles, etc.).
  • Spinal injuries (fractures, disc disease, surgery, instrumentation).
  • Recurrent dislocations (shoulder, patella, etc.).
  • Previous ICU or High-Care admissions due to trauma.

Previous ICU / High-Care Admissions

  • Always ask if the patient has been in ICU or High-Care previously.
  • Clarify why they were admitted (sepsis, trauma, respiratory failure, cardiac event, etc.).
  • Ask about intubation, ventilation, inotropes, and complications (e.g. pressure sores, AKI).

Quick ED Co-Morbidity Checklist

  • Diabetes, Hypertension, HIV, TB, Asthma/COPD, Epilepsy.
  • Heart disease (IHD, heart failure, arrhythmia, valve disease).
  • Renal or liver disease, dialysis history.
  • Previous stroke, neurosurgery, space-occupying lesions.
  • Major surgeries, amputations, ICU/High-Care admissions.
  • Current medications and allergies (especially anticoagulants, steroids, ART).
