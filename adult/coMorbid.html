Co-Morbidities

  • Diabetes Type I
  • Diabetes Type II
  • Epilepsy
  • Asthma
  • TB
    • Important!!! Make sure you can look at the TB DOTS (green) Card , or equivalent in your setting.
    • It gives you the timeline, dosages, changes and complications.
    • If you cannot see it, make sure you try and get as much information about this from the patient
    • And differentiate the type of TB
      • Pulmonary
      • Abdominal
      • Joint
      • Spinal
      • Meningitis
      • Intra-cranial
  • Hypertension
  • Other Medical Conditions
    • The Ffg is a concise (but not exhaustive) list of the most common Medical conditions you should ask about
    • Ischemic Heart Disease
    • Congestive Cardiac Failure
    • Valvular Heart Disease
    • Rheumatic heart Disease
    • Known Arrhythmias
    • COPD
    • Pneumonia
    • Lung Cancer
    • Renal Impairment
      • Its good to know if someone has had to be on dialysis or diuretics or chelating agents.
    • Liver Disease
      • Cirrhosis
      • Cancer
      • Cysts and Lesions
      • Includes Gallbladder disease
    • Previous CVA
    • Space Occupying Lesions
      • In the neurological diseases it is important to know the patients baseline function.
    • Its very important to know about previous admissions (especially in the ICU or High-care).
  • Other Surgical Diagnosis
    • The Ffg is a concise (but not exhaustive) list of the most common Surgical conditions you should ask about
    • Previous Abdominal Surgery
    • Appendicitis
    • VPrevious traumatic Brain Injury
    • Hyper/ Hypothyroidism
    • GIT Ulcerations
    • COPD
    • Bowel Obstruction
    • Lung Cancer
    • Hernias
      • Its good to know if has had previous incarcerations or strangulations.
    • Deep Vein Thrombosis
    • Peripheral Vascular Disease
    • Amputations
