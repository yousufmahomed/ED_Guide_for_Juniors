Antidysrhythmic Drugs

Class Drug Dosage Route of Administration Indication for Use
Class I Lidocaine (Xylocaine) 1-1.5 mg/kg bolus followed by 0.5-0.75 mg/kg every 5-10 minutes as needed Intravenous Ventricular tachycardia, ventricular fibrillation
Procainamide (Procan, Procanbid) 20-30 mg/min, maximum dose 17 mg/kg Intravenous Ventricular tachycardia, atrial fibrillation, atrial flutter
Class II Propranolol (Inderal) 1-3 mg intravenously over 2-3 minutes, then repeat every 2-5 minutes as needed; maximum dose of 10 mg Intravenous Supraventricular tachycardia, ventricular arrhythmias
Metoprolol (Lopressor) 5 mg intravenously over 1-2 minutes, repeat every 5 minutes as needed; maximum dose of 15 mg Intravenous Supraventricular tachycardia, ventricular arrhythmias
Class III Amiodarone (Cordarone, Pacerone) Initial loading dose of 150-300 mg over 10-20 minutes, followed by a maintenance infusion of 1 mg/min for 6 hours, then 0.5 mg/min for 18 hours Intravenous Ventricular arrhythmias, atrial fibrillation, atrial flutter
Ibutilide (Corvert) 1 mg over 10 minutes; may repeat in 10 minutes at a dose of 0.5 mg if necessary Intravenous Atrial fibrillation, atrial flutter
Class IV Verapamil (Calan, Isoptin) 5-10 mg intravenously over 2-3 minutes; may repeat in 30 minutes if necessary Intravenous Supraventricular tachycardia
Diltiazem (Cardizem) 0.25 mg/kg intravenously over 2 minutes, followed by an infusion of 5-15 mg/hour Intravenous Supraventricular tachycardia
First Line Adenosine (Adenocard) 6 mg rapid intravenous push followed by a 20 mL saline flush; if no response after 1-2 minutes, may administer a 12 mg dose followed by a 20 mL saline flush Intravenous Supraventricular tachycardia
