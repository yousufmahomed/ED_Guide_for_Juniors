Osmolality Calculator

Indication/Abnormality Definition Interpretation Clinical Implications
Osmolality The concentration of solutes in a solution, measured in milliosmoles per kilogram (mOsm/kg) Normal range: 275-295 mOsm/kg Used to evaluate hydration status and diagnose electrolyte imbalances, such as dehydration, overhydration, hypernatremia, and hyponatremia.
High Osmolality Elevated concentration of solutes in the blood May indicate dehydration, hypernatremia, or other electrolyte imbalances Requires further evaluation to determine the underlying cause and appropriate treatment, such as fluid replacement or electrolyte correction.
Low Osmolality Decreased concentration of solutes in the blood May indicate overhydration, hyponatremia, or other electrolyte imbalances Requires further evaluation to determine the underlying cause and appropriate treatment, such as fluid restriction or electrolyte supplementation.
