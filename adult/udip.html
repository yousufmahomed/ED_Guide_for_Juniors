Urine Dipstick Test Guide

Urine Dipstick Test Parameters and Interpretation

Parameter Normal Value Possible Abnormalities Common Causes and Further Investigations
Leukocytes Negative Positive Infection (e.g., urinary tract infection), inflammation, kidney stones; Consider urine culture, microscopy, and sensitivity
Nitrite Negative Positive Bacterial infection (e.g., urinary tract infection); Consider urine culture, microscopy, and sensitivity
Urobilinogen 0.2 - 1.0 mg/dL Increased or Decreased Increased: hemolysis, liver disease; Decreased: biliary obstruction; Consider liver function tests, hemoglobin, and imaging (e.g., ultrasound)
Protein Negative to trace Positive Renal disease (e.g., glomerulonephritis), urinary tract infection, dehydration, diabetes, hypertension, pre-eclampsia in pregnancy; Consider renal function tests, urine protein-to-creatinine ratio, 24-hour urine protein, and kidney ultrasound
pH 4.5 - 8.0 Increased or Decreased Increased: urinary tract infection, renal tubular acidosis, chronic vomiting; Decreased: metabolic acidosis, uncontrolled diabetes, diarrhea; Consider arterial blood gas, blood glucose, and electrolyte tests
Blood Negative Positive Infection, kidney stones, urinary tract injury, bladder or kidney cancer, medications; Consider urine microscopy, imaging (e.g., ultrasound, CT), and cystoscopy
Specific Gravity 1.005 - 1.030 Increased or Decreased Increased: dehydration, heart failure, liver disease; Decreased: overhydration, diabetes insipidus, renal failure; Consider renal function tests, fluid balance assessment, and imaging (e.g., ultrasound)
Ketones Negative Positive Diabetes, starvation, prolonged vomiting, low carbohydrate diet; Consider blood glucose, HbA1c, and blood ketone tests
Glucose Negative Positive Diabetes, stress, certain medications; Consider blood glucose, HbA1c, and oral glucose tolerance test
Bilirubin Negative Positive Liver disease, biliary obstruction, hemolysis; Consider liver function tests, imaging (e.g., ultrasound), and hemoglobin test
