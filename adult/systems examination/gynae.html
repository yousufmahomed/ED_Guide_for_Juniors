Gynecological Examination

Gynecological Examination in the Emergency Department

A gynecological examination is performed to evaluate a woman's reproductive system. In the emergency department, it is often performed to assess for conditions such as pelvic inflammatory disease, ectopic pregnancy, and ovarian cysts.

Preparation

Before the examination, the patient should be positioned comfortably on the examination table and provided with a sheet or gown for modesty. The patient should be informed about the procedure and given the opportunity to ask questions or voice concerns.

Components of the Examination

  • Inspection: Assess for any external abnormalities such as lesions, inflammation, or discharge.
  • Speculum examination: Insertion of a speculum into the vagina to visualize the cervix and assess for any abnormalities such as cervical discharge or lesions. Samples of vaginal fluid may be collected for testing for sexually transmitted infections, if indicated.
  • Bimanual examination: Insertion of two fingers into the vagina while the other hand is placed on the abdomen to assess the size and position of the uterus and ovaries. This can be helpful in identifying conditions such as ovarian cysts or pelvic inflammatory disease.

Diagnostic Tests

Depending on the patient's symptoms and physical exam findings, diagnostic tests may include:

  • Pregnancy test
  • STI testing
  • Pelvic ultrasound
  • Blood tests

Differential Diagnosis

Conditions that may present with gynecological symptoms in the emergency department include:

Condition Common Symptoms Diagnostic Tests Treatment Danger Signs Grading
Pelvic inflammatory disease Pelvic pain, fever, vaginal discharge Pelvic exam, cervical swabs, ultrasound Antibiotics, pain management High fever, severe abdominal pain, rapid heartbeat Mild, moderate, severe (based on clinical presentation and laboratory results)
Ectopic pregnancy Abdominal pain, vaginal bleeding, missed period Pregnancy test, ultrasound, laparoscopy Surgery or medication to terminate pregnancy, pain management Severe abdominal pain, dizziness or lightheadedness, shoulder pain, vaginal bleeding with clotting Based on size and location of ectopic pregnancy
Endometriosis Pelvic pain, painful periods, painful intercourse Pelvic exam, ultrasound, laparoscopy Pain management, hormonal therapy, surgery Severe pelvic pain, heavy bleeding, fever, rapid heartbeat Stage 1-4 (based on extent and severity of endometriosis lesions)
Urinary tract infection Burning with urination, frequent urination, pelvic pain Urinalysis, urine culture, imaging studies Antibiotics, pain management Fever, nausea, vomiting, chills, severe abdominal pain Mild, moderate, severe (based on clinical presentation and laboratory results)
