Genitourinary Exam

Genitourinary System Examination

The genitourinary system includes the kidneys, ureters, bladder, urethra, prostate (in males), and reproductive organs (in both males and females). A comprehensive examination includes a history, physical examination, and diagnostic tests if necessary.

History

Ask the patient about any symptoms they are experiencing, such as pain with urination, changes in urinary frequency, urgency, or incontinence, blood in the urine, flank pain, or genital or pelvic pain. Also ask about any past medical history, such as kidney disease, urinary tract infections, sexually transmitted infections, or prostate problems.

Physical Examination

Male Examination

For male patients, the examination may include:

  • Inspection of the external genitalia for any lesions, swelling, or discharge
  • Palpation of the testes for size, tenderness, or masses
  • Palpation of the prostate for size, tenderness, or nodules
  • Digital rectal examination to assess for any rectal masses or abnormalities

Female Examination

For female patients, the examination may include:

  • Inspection of the external genitalia for any lesions, swelling, or discharge
  • Bimanual examination to assess the size, position, and tenderness of the uterus and ovaries
  • Pelvic examination to assess for any masses or tenderness
  • Speculum examination to inspect the cervix and collect samples for testing (such as a Pap smear)

Diagnostic Tests

Diagnostic tests may include:

  • Urinalysis to assess for infection, blood, or other abnormalities
  • Urine culture to identify any bacterial infections
  • Blood tests to assess kidney function
  • Imaging studies, such as ultrasound or CT scan, to assess for any abnormalities in the kidneys or bladder
  • Cystoscopy to visualize the bladder and urethra

Conclusion

The genitourinary system examination is an important aspect of the Emergency Department evaluation, particularly when patients present with symptoms related to urination, genital or pelvic pain, or other concerning findings. A thorough history and physical examination, along with appropriate diagnostic testing, can help identify potential problems and guide appropriate treatment.
