Knee Exam

Introduction

A knee examination is performed in the emergency department to evaluate injuries or abnormalities involving the knee joint. This assessment is crucial for identifying ligament, tendon, cartilage, or bony injuries and directing appropriate imaging and management.

Examination Technique

The knee examination involves the following steps:

  1. Inspect the knee for swelling, bruising, deformity, wounds, or penetrating injuries.
  2. Palpate for tenderness, warmth, effusion, crepitus, and bony step-offs.
  3. Assess range of motion: flexion, extension, and patellar tracking.
  4. Perform ligamentous tests: Lachman (ACL), valgus/varus stress tests (MCL/LCL), posterior sag (PCL), and pivot shift (ACL).
  5. Perform meniscal tests: McMurray, Thessaly, joint line tenderness.
  6. Perform a neurovascular exam: sensation, motor, distal pulses.
  7. Observe gait for limping, instability, inability to fully extend, or quadriceps avoidance.

Common Knee Injuries and Conditions

Some common knee injuries and conditions include:

Injury/Condition Signs and Symptoms Differential Diagnosis Treatment
ACL Tear Swelling, instability, popping sensation, difficulty weight-bearing MCL tear, meniscal tear, patellar dislocation RICE, physio, bracing, surgical reconstruction
MCL Tear Medial knee pain, valgus instability LCL tear, medial meniscus tear RICE, physio, hinged brace; surgery if severe
Meniscal Tear Locking, clicking, joint line tenderness ACL tear, OA flare RICE, physio, arthroscopy
Patellar Tendinopathy Inferior patellar pain, pain on jumping Patellofemoral syndrome Rest, eccentric loading rehab
Baker's Cyst Posterior swelling, tightness, pain DVT, meniscal tear Conservative management, aspiration

Treatment

Management is based on severity and underlying pathology. Options include:

  • Rest, ice, compression, elevation (RICE)
  • NSAIDs and analgesia
  • Physiotherapy and gradual strengthening
  • Bracing for ligament injuries
  • Surgical repair or reconstruction when indicated

Prevention

Preventive measures include:

  • Strengthening quadriceps and hamstrings
  • Proper footwear
  • Warm-up and stretching
  • Good landing mechanics for jumping sports
