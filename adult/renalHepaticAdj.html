Medication Adjustment for Renal and Hepatic Impairment

Patients with renal or hepatic impairment may require adjustment of medication dosages in order to avoid adverse effects or toxicity. Dosage adjustment is typically calculated based on the degree of impairment, as measured by laboratory values such as creatinine clearance or liver function tests.

Commonly Adjusted Medications

Medication Adjustment for Renal Impairment Adjustment for Hepatic Impairment
Metformin Reduce dose or avoid use in severe renal impairment (GFR < 30 mL/min) Avoid use in severe hepatic impairment
Warfarin Reduce dose or avoid use in moderate to severe renal impairment (GFR < 50 mL/min) Reduce dose in mild to moderate hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh A or B)
Lisinopril Reduce dose or avoid use in severe renal impairment (GFR < 30 mL/min) No dosage adjustment necessary
Ceftriaxone Reduce dose or prolong dosing interval in renal impairment No dosage adjustment necessary
Vancomycin Adjust dose based on renal function (CrCl or GFR) Adjust dose based on liver function tests (AST, ALT, bilirubin)
Azithromycin No dosage adjustment necessary Avoid use in severe hepatic impairment

Formulas for Medication Dosage Adjustment

Doctors can use the following formulas to adjust medication dosages based on renal or hepatic function:

  • Creatinine clearance (CrCl) = (140 - age) x weight (kg) / (72 x serum creatinine (mg/dL))
  • Child-Pugh score = (1.0 x bilirubin (mg/dL)) + (0.7 x INR) + (1.8 x ascites) + (3.0 x encephalopathy)

To adjust medication dosages using the CrCl value:

  1. Determine the target dose of the medication based on the patient's condition and other factors.
  2. Calculate the patient's estimated CrCl value using the formula.
  3. Use a drug-specific dosing algorithm or table to determine the appropriate dose adjustment based on the estimated CrCl value.
  4. Administer the medication at the adjusted dose.

To adjust medication dosages using the Child-Pugh score:

  1. Determine the target dose of the medication based on the patient's condition and other factors.
  2. Calculate the patient's Child-Pugh score using the formula.
  3. Use a drug-specific dosing algorithm or table to determine the appropriate dose adjustment based on the patient's Child-Pugh score.
  4. Administer the medication at the adjusted dose.

It's important to note that medication adjustment based on renal and hepatic function is typically done in consultation with a Senior Colleague or Pharmacist, as many other factors may need to be taken into account when adjusting medication dosages.
