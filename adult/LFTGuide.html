LFT Guide

Liver Function Tests

Liver function tests (LFTs) are a group of blood tests that are used to assess the liver's overall health and function. These tests can help identify liver damage or disease, monitor the effectiveness of treatment, and detect the presence of liver cancer.

Types of Liver Function Tests

There are several types of liver function tests that may be ordered by a doctor:

  • Alanine aminotransferase (ALT): measures the level of ALT, an enzyme found primarily in the liver. Elevated levels may indicate liver damage or disease, such as hepatitis or cirrhosis.
  • Aspartate aminotransferase (AST): measures the level of AST, an enzyme found in the liver, heart, muscles, and other tissues. Elevated levels may indicate liver damage or disease, but may also be caused by heart disease or muscle damage.
  • Alkaline phosphatase (ALP): measures the level of ALP, an enzyme found in the liver, bile ducts, and bones. Elevated levels may indicate liver or bone disease, or blockage of the bile ducts.
  • Total bilirubin (TBIL): measures the level of bilirubin, a yellowish substance produced when red blood cells break down. Elevated levels may indicate liver damage or disease, or obstruction of the bile ducts.
  • Albumin: measures the level of albumin, a protein produced by the liver. Low levels may indicate liver damage or disease, malnutrition, or kidney disease.
  • Prothrombin time (PT): measures the time it takes for blood to clot. Elevated levels may indicate liver damage or disease, as the liver produces many of the proteins necessary for blood clotting.
  • Gamma-glutamyl transferase (GGT): measures the level of GGT, an enzyme found in the liver and other tissues. Elevated levels may indicate liver damage or disease, or excessive alcohol consumption.

Interpreting Liver Function Tests

Interpreting liver function test results can be complex, and requires careful consideration of multiple factors, such as the patient's medical history, current symptoms, and other diagnostic tests. However, here are some general guidelines for interpreting LFTs:

Parameter Terminology for Increase Terminology for Decrease Differential Diagnosis for Increase Differential Diagnosis for Decrease
Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT) Elevated ALT (> 50 U/L) Low ALT (< 7 U/L) Hepatitis, liver injury, liver disease, alcohol abuse, drug toxicity Malnutrition, low protein diet, vitamin B6 deficiency
Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST) Elevated AST (> 40 U/L) Low AST (< 5 U/L) Hepatitis, liver injury, liver disease, alcohol abuse, drug toxicity Malnutrition, low protein diet
Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP) Elevated ALP (> 130 U/L) Low ALP (< 20 U/L) Liver disease, biliary obstruction, bone disease, healing fractures, pregnancy Vitamin D deficiency, malnutrition, hypothyroidism
Bilirubin Elevated bilirubin (> 20 µmol/L) Low bilirubin (< 4 µmol/L) Liver disease, bile duct obstruction, hemolytic anemia, gallstones, hepatitis, cirrhosis
Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase (GGT) Elevated GGT (> 60 U/L) Low GGT (< 5 U/L) Alcohol abuse, liver disease, bile duct obstruction, drug toxicity, heart failure
Albumin Low albumin (< 35 g/L) High albumin (> 50 g/L) Liver disease, malnutrition, kidney disease, inflammation, cancer, heart failure Dehydration, overhydration, pregnancy
