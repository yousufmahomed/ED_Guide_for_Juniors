Anemia Diagnosis Guide

Overview

Anemia is a condition characterized by a decrease in the number of red blood cells (RBCs) or a reduction in the hemoglobin (Hb) content of the blood. This results in reduced oxygen-carrying capacity and can lead to various symptoms, such as fatigue, weakness, pallor, and shortness of breath.

Initial Assessment

  1. Obtain a detailed history: symptoms, duration, family history, diet, medications, etc.
  2. Perform a physical examination: pallor, jaundice, tachycardia, hepatosplenomegaly, etc.
  3. Order relevant laboratory tests: complete blood count (CBC), reticulocyte count, peripheral blood smear, and iron studies.

Classification of Anemia

Anemia can be classified based on the mean corpuscular volume (MCV) of the RBCs:

  • Microcytic anemia (MCV < 80 fL)
  • Normocytic anemia (MCV 80-100 fL)
  • Macrocytic anemia (MCV > 100 fL)

Diagnosis of Different Types of Anemia

Type of Anemia MCV Key Laboratory Findings Causes
Iron Deficiency Anemia Microcytic (MCV < 80 fL) Low serum iron, low ferritin, high total iron-binding capacity (TIBC) Poor diet, malabsorption, blood loss, pregnancy
Thalassemia Microcytic (MCV < 80 fL) Normal to high serum iron, normal to high ferritin, normal to low TIBC, hemoglobin electrophoresis may show abnormal Hb patterns Genetic mutation affecting hemoglobin synthesis
Anemia of Chronic Disease Normocytic (MCV 80-100 fL) or Microcytic (MCV < 80 fL) Low serum iron, high ferritin, low TIBC Chronic infections, inflammation, malignancy
Macrocytic Anemia Macrocytic (MCV > 100 fL) Low serum B12 or folate, hypersegmented neutrophils on peripheral blood smear Vitamin B12 or folate deficiency, alcoholism, liver disease
Hemolytic Anemia Normocytic (MCV 80-100 fL) Reticulocytosis, increased indirect bilirubin, increased lactate dehydrogenase (LDH), decreased haptoglobin Intrinsic RBC defects, autoimmune destruction, mechanical damage, infections
Aplastic Anemia Normocytic (MCV 80-100 fL) Pancytopenia, low reticulocyte count, hypocellular bone marrow Idiopathic, drug toxicity, radiation, viral infections, inherited bone marrow failure syndromes

Treatment

Treatment of anemia depends on the underlying cause. It is essential to address the root cause and provide supportive care. This may include iron, vitamin B12, or folate supplementation, blood transfusions, immunosuppressive therapy, or other specific treatments depending on the type of anemia and its severity.
