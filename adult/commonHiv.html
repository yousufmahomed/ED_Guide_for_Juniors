Commonly Used HIV Drugs

Drug Dosage Route of Administration Indication for Use
Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF) 300 mg once daily Oral HIV-1 infection in adults and pediatric patients 2 years of age and older
Emtricitabine (FTC) 200 mg once daily Oral HIV-1 infection in adults and pediatric patients 2 years of age and older, pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP)
Dolutegravir (DTG) 50 mg once daily Oral HIV-1 infection in adults and pediatric patients 12 years of age and older
Raltegravir (RAL) 400 mg twice daily Oral HIV-1 infection in adults and pediatric patients 4 weeks of age and older
Atazanavir (ATV) 300-400 mg once daily with ritonavir 100 mg once daily or 200 mg once daily (if ATV is co-administered with tenofovir disoproxil fumarate) Oral HIV-1 infection in adults and pediatric patients 6 years of age and older
Ritonavir (RTV) 100 mg once daily or twice daily Oral HIV-1 infection in adults and pediatric patients
Darunavir (DRV) 800 mg once daily with ritonavir 100 mg once daily or 600 mg twice daily with ritonavir 100 mg twice daily Oral HIV-1 infection in adults and pediatric patients 3 years of age and older
Lopinavir/ritonavir (LPV/r) 800/200 mg once or twice daily Oral HIV-1 infection in adults and pediatric patients
