Commonly Used Analgesics in Hospitals

Analgesic Dosage Route of Administration
Acetaminophen (Paracetamol) 500–1000 mg every 6 hours (max 4 g/day) Oral, IV, rectal
Aspirin 300–600 mg every 4–6 hours Oral
Diclofenac Sodium 50–75 mg every 8–12 hours Oral, intramuscular
Fentanyl 25–100 mcg every 1–2 hours IV, transdermal patch, transmucosal
Ibuprofen 200–400 mg every 6 hours (max 2400 mg/day) Oral
Ketorolac * 10–30 mg every 6 hours (max 5 days) IV, IM
Morphine 2.5–10 mg every 4 hours titrated IV, IM, SC
Pethidine (Meperidine) ** 50–75 mg every 4–6 hours IM, IV
Tramadol 50–100 mg every 6 hours (max 400 mg/day) Oral, IV
Oxycodone 5–10 mg every 4–6 hours Oral, IV

* Ketorolac: Avoid in renal impairment, elderly, bleeding risk, peptic ulcer disease.

** Pethidine: Avoid high doses due to risk of seizures (normeperidine). Do NOT exceed 75 mg without senior approval.
