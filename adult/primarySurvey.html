Primary Survey

Primary Survey of an Emergency Patient

Introduction

The primary survey is the initial assessment of an emergency patient to identify and address any life-threatening conditions. The primary survey follows the ABCDE approach:

  • A: Airway
  • B: Breathing
  • C: Circulation
  • D: Disability
  • E: Exposure/Environment

Airway

Clinical features: Inability to speak, stridor, gurgling, cyanosis

Symptoms: Shortness of breath, difficulty breathing, hoarseness

Causes: Obstruction, injury, infection, inflammation

Relevant interventions: Head tilt-chin lift, jaw thrust, suction, intubation

Indications for Intubation Confirmatory Tests for Placement Reasons for Desaturation
Severe respiratory distress End-tidal CO2 monitoring, chest x-ray Airway obstruction, hypoventilation
Apnea or impending respiratory failure Direct laryngoscopy, bronchoscopy Inadequate oxygenation or ventilation
Protecting the airway in unconscious or sedated patients Capnography, ultrasound Pneumothorax, aspiration, tube dislodgement
Facilitating mechanical ventilation Auscultation, chest rise and fall, pulse oximetry Tension pneumothorax, equipment malfunction

Breathing

Clinical features: Rapid breathing, shallow breathing, noisy breathing, chest wall injury

Symptoms: Shortness of breath, wheezing, coughing, chest pain

Causes: Obstruction, injury, infection, inflammation, lung disease

Relevant interventions: Oxygen therapy, ventilation, chest tube insertion, needle decompression

Circulation

Clinical features: Rapid heart rate, low blood pressure, pallor, cyanosis

Symptoms: Weakness, dizziness, confusion, chest pain

Causes: Hypovolemia, shock, cardiac arrest, arrhythmia

Relevant interventions: Fluid resuscitation, blood transfusion, medication (e.g. epinephrine)

Disability

Clinical features: Altered mental status, focal neurologic deficits, pupil abnormalities

Symptoms: Headache, weakness, numbness, tingling

Causes: Trauma, stroke, seizure, drug overdose, hypoglycemia

Relevant interventions: Glasgow Coma Scale assessment, neurological exam, treatment of underlying cause

Exposure/Environment

Clinical features: Hypothermia, hyperthermia, burns, frostbite

Symptoms: Shivering, sweating, pain, numbness

Causes: Environmental exposure, chemical exposure, radiation exposure

Relevant interventions: Temperature management, decontamination, removal from source of exposure
