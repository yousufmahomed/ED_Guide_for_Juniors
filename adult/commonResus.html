Commonly Used Resuscitation Drugs

Drug Dosage Route of Administration Indication for Use
Adrenaline (Epinephrine) 1 mg every 3-5 minutes Intravenous, intraosseous, or endotracheal Cardiac arrest, anaphylaxis, bronchospasm, hypotension
Amiodarone 300 mg bolus, then 150 mg over 10 minutes Intravenous Ventricular fibrillation, ventricular tachycardia
Atropine 0.5-1 mg every 3-5 minutes Intravenous, intraosseous, or endotracheal Bradycardia, organophosphate poisoning
Bicarbonate 50-100 mEq Intravenous Metabolic acidosis, hyperkalemia
Lidocaine 1-1.5 mg/kg bolus, then 0.5-0.75 mg/kg every 5-10 minutes Intravenous, intraosseous, or endotracheal Ventricular fibrillation, ventricular tachycardia
Calcium gluconate 1 g (10 mL of 10% solution) over 10-15 minutes Intravenous Hyperkalemia, hypocalcemia, calcium channel blocker overdose
Furosemide 20-40 mg intravenous or intramuscular Intravenous or intramuscular Acute pulmonary edema, edema associated with heart failure, hypertension
Hydrocortisone 100-300 mg/day Intravenous or intramuscular Adrenal insufficiency, sepsis, anaphylaxis, asthma
Short-acting insulin Based on blood glucose levels Subcutaneous or intravenous Diabetic ketoacidosis, hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state, hyperkalemia
Ipratropium 0.5 mg (1 vial) mixed with 2.5-3 mL of normal saline, administered every 20 minutes up to 3 doses Inhalation Acute exacerbation of asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
Magnesium sulfate 2-4 g intravenous over 5-20 minutes Intravenous Torsades de pointes, eclampsia, hypomagnesemia
Promethazine 12.5-25 mg intravenous or intramuscular Intravenous or intramuscular Nausea and vomiting, motion sickness, allergic reactions
Sublingual nitrates 0.3-0.6 mg every 5 minutes up to 3 doses Sublingual Angina pectoris, acute myocardial infarction
Thiamine 100 mg intravenous or intramuscular Intravenous or intramuscular Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome, alcohol withdrawal, malnutrition
Salbutamol (Albuterol) 2.5-5 mg nebulized or 4-8 puffs via metered-dose inhaler every 20 minutes up to 3 doses Inhalation Acute exacerbation of asthma or COPD, Hyperkalemia
Naloxone 20.4-2 mg intravenous, intramuscular, or subcutaneous, repeat every 2-3 minutes as needed Intravenous, intramuscular, or subcutaneous Opioid overdose, respiratory depression due to opioids
50% Dextrose 25 g (50 mL of 50% solution) intravenous, may repeat once if needed IIntravenous Hypoglycemia
Labetalol 20 mg intravenous over 2 minutes, may repeat every 10 minutes up to 300 mg total Intravenous Hypertensive emergency, preeclampsia, tachycardia
