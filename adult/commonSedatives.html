⚠️ Important Safety Notes for ED Sedation

  • Always monitor: cardiac monitor, SpO₂, BP, and end-tidal CO₂ if available.
  • Never give sedatives without resus equipment at bedside—airway trolley, suction, oxygen, BVM.
  • Propofol requires senior supervision: high risk of apnea, hypotension, and airway loss.
  • Ketamine preserves airway reflexes but hypersalivation, laryngospasm, and hypertension can occur.
  • Midazolam has highest risk of respiratory depression—titrate very slowly.
  • Always consider reversible causes of agitation (hypoxia, hypoglycaemia, drugs, sepsis).
  • This table is a quick reference — not a substitute for local ED sedation protocols.
