Obgyn History

Menstrual history

This includes the age at which the patient started menstruating, the length of their menstrual cycle, and the duration and flow of their periods.
It is also important to ask about any irregularities or changes in the patient's menstrual cycle.

Obstetric history

This includes the number of pregnancies and live births, as well as any miscarriages, abortions, or stillbirths.
It is also important to ask about any complications during pregnancy, labor, or delivery.

Contraceptive history

This includes the type of contraception used by the patient, including hormonal methods, barrier methods, or intrauterine devices (IUDs).
It is also important to ask about any prior contraceptive failures or side effects.

Sexual history

This includes the patient's sexual orientation, number of partners, and any history of sexually transmitted infections (STIs).
It is also important to ask about any pain, discomfort, or changes in sexual function.

Gynecological history

This includes any prior gynecologic conditions, such as infections, fibroids, polyps, or endometriosis.
It is also important to ask about any surgeries or procedures related to the reproductive system.

Family history

This includes any history of gynecologic or breast cancer in the patient's immediate family.

Expected Delivery Date Calculator

Yes, the expected delivery date (EDD) calculated based on the first day of the last menstrual period (LMP) can be used to estimate the expected symphysis-fundal height (SFH).

The SFH measurement is a non-invasive method used to assess fetal growth during pregnancy. It is measured from the top of the pubic bone (symphysis pubis) to the top of the uterus (fundus) and is usually expressed in centimeters. The measurement is taken regularly during prenatal care appointments to monitor fetal growth and ensure that it is consistent with the gestational age.

Typically, the SFH measurement is expected to correspond to the gestational age of the fetus. For example, if the EDD calculated based on the LMP is January 8, 2023, then the expected gestational age on August 1, 2022, would be approximately 24 weeks. Therefore, the expected SFH measurement for a 24-week gestational age would be around 24 cm, although this can vary based on factors such as maternal body habitus, fetal position, and amniotic fluid volume.
