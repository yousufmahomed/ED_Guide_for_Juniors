Pediatric Respiratory Exam

Pediatric Respiratory Examination in the Emergency Department

A pediatric respiratory examination is essential for evaluating children presenting to the emergency department with respiratory symptoms. This examination aims to identify respiratory abnormalities affecting the lungs, airways, and chest wall.

Inspection

Begin with inspection to observe breathing patterns, chest movements, and signs of respiratory distress:

  • Assess breathing rate, rhythm, and depth.
  • Look for retractions or nasal flaring indicating increased work of breathing.
  • Check for chest wall deformities or asymmetry.
  • Observe for cyanosis of lips, tongue, or fingers.

Palpation

Palpate the chest and neck areas to assess respiratory function:

  • Check for tenderness, crepitus, or masses on the chest wall.
  • Assess breath sounds for abnormalities like decreased or increased sounds.
  • Palpate trachea for any deviation indicating airway obstruction.
  • Assess neck for tenderness or crepitus, suggesting subcutaneous emphysema.

Percussion

Use percussion to assess lung tissue density:

  • Percuss chest wall for dullness (consolidation, pleural effusion) or hyperresonance (pneumothorax, emphysema).

Auscultation

Listen for breath sounds and abnormalities using a stethoscope:

  • Auscultate anterior and posterior chest walls for normal/abnormal breath sounds, wheezing, crackles, or rhonchi.
  • Auscultate trachea and mainstem bronchi for breath sound abnormalities and stridor.

Common Pediatric Respiratory Illnesses

Respiratory Illness Clinical Presentation
Asthma
  • Wheezing during expiration
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cough, especially at night or early morning
  • Increase in symptoms with triggers (allergens, exercise)
Pneumonia
  • Fever, chills
  • Cough with or without mucus
  • Rapid or difficult breathing
  • Chest pain
Croup
  • Barking cough
  • Stridor
  • Hoarseness
  • Difficulty breathing

Conclusion

A pediatric respiratory examination is vital for assessing children with respiratory symptoms. By inspecting, palpating, percussing, and auscultating, healthcare providers can identify abnormalities and develop appropriate treatment plans. Early detection and intervention ensure optimal outcomes for pediatric patients.
