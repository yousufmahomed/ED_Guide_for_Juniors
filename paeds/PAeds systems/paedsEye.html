Pediatric Ophthalmology Exam

Conduct a comprehensive eye and vision exam tailored for pediatric patients, including:

Visual Assessment

Evaluate the child's visual acuity using child-friendly eye charts or methods:

Visual acuity testing assesses a child's ability to see objects clearly at a distance. It involves using age-appropriate charts or interactive methods to measure how well the child can identify shapes, letters, or symbols.

The results are typically recorded as "20/20" if the child can see at a standard distance what a person with normal vision can see at 20 feet. Adjustments can be made for children who are unable to read letters by using pictures or other engaging visual stimuli.

External Eye Examination

Perform an external examination of the eyes, including:

  • Redness, swelling, or discharge: These symptoms may indicate infections or inflammation.
  • Eyelid position and movement: Assess for any abnormalities in eyelid control or movement.
  • Lumps or bumps: Look for any growths or masses that may affect eye health.
  • Foreign bodies: Check for foreign objects that may have entered the eye.

Internal Eye Examination

Conduct an internal eye examination using specialized tools, including:

  • Pupillary response: Evaluate pupil size and light reaction for signs of nerve function.
  • Lens clarity: Check the transparency of the lens, looking for cloudiness or abnormalities.
  • Retina and optic nerve: Examine the retina and optic nerve for signs of abnormalities.

Intraocular Pressure

Measure the child's intraocular pressure using child-friendly methods. Elevated or low intraocular pressure may have different implications in children than in adults.

Types of Pediatric Eye Conditions

Type of Condition Common Clinical Features
Amblyopia (Lazy Eye)
  • Reduced vision in one eye
  • Strabismus (crossed or misaligned eyes)
  • Squinting or tilting the head to see
  • Difficulty with depth perception
Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye)
  • Redness and irritation of the eye
  • Watery or mucous discharge
  • Crusty eyelids in the morning
  • Itching or discomfort
Pre-septal Cellulitis
  • Swelling and redness around the eye
  • Tenderness and warmth in the affected area
  • Eye pain or discomfort
  • Fever
  • No visual changes or loss of vision
Orbital Cellulitis
  • Swelling and redness around the eye
  • Proptosis (bulging of the eye)
  • Pain with eye movements
  • Decreased or blurry vision
  • Double vision
  • Fever
  • Eye pain or discomfort
  • Tenderness and warmth in the affected area
  • Decreased eye movement or inability to move the eye
