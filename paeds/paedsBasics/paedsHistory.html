Pediatric Medical History

Main Complaint

This is a brief statement by the caregiver/parent that describes the reason for seeking medical attention for the child. It is important to document their own words.

  • Try and elicit as many symptoms as you can, as this will help you in diagnosis.
  • Be open to listening, the caregiver/parent is not a health professional, and you may have to read between the lines, considering their observations and concerns to understand the child's condition better.
  • This may sound intimidating, but as you take more histories and practice, it can be accomplished in a very short time.
  • It is also important to understand the circumstances surrounding the illness or injury.
    • For example, if the child has an injury, try to gather information about the event that led to the injury to better assess the condition.

History of Present Illness

This is a detailed description of the current problem the child is experiencing.

  • Onset
    • Is this the first time the child is experiencing this problem, or has it occurred before?
    • If it is recurring, try to determine if this episode is similar to others, and if not, how does it differ?
  • Duration of symptoms
    • If the problem has been present for more than 24 hours, what prompted the caregiver/parent to seek medical help now?
    • Consider factors that led to seeking medical attention, such as changes in severity, lack of improvement with home care, or specific events or symptoms concerning the caregiver/parent.
  • Severity (Use appropriate scales if needed)
    • Has the severity changed over time, and if so, how?
    • Ask about factors that aggravate or alleviate the symptoms to get a better understanding of the child's condition.

Past Medical & Co-Morbid History

This includes a summary of the child's past illnesses, surgeries, hospitalizations, and chronic conditions.

For a more comprehensive look at this aspect, please ask the caregiver/parent about the child's medical history and any significant medical conditions the child has experienced in the past.

Medications

Document all current medications the child is taking, including prescription medications, over-the-counter medications, and supplements.

It is also important to note medications that the child may have taken for long periods before but have now stopped and why.

Ask the caregiver/parent about the frequency and dosages of any medications the child is currently or was previously taking.

Hint!! If the caregiver/parent has brought the child's medications along (or a prescription or list), thank them for their thoughtfulness, as it builds rapport and makes the consultation go much smoother.

Allergies

Document any known allergies the child has.

Especially important are allergies to medications, food, or other substances that may cause allergic reactions in the child.

Family History

Document any significant medical problems or conditions that run in the child's family.

Ask the caregiver/parent about immediate family members' medical history, especially if there are any conditions that have caused deaths or extended treatments.

For example, knowing that the child has a family history of certain diseases can be important for early diagnosis and prevention.

Social History

This includes information on the child's living situation, any relevant social habits of the family (e.g., smoking or alcohol use by family members), and the child's usual daily activities.

Events Preceding Illness/Injury

This is more important for patients where the mechanism of injury or illness seems suspicious or warrants further investigation.

For example, if a child presents with an injury, ask detailed questions about the events leading up to the injury to rule out any potential child abuse or trauma.

Child's Last Meal

This is especially important if the child requires a medical procedure or surgery.

It is impossible to cover all the intricacies of history taking for pediatric patients on a single page, and you should refer to your Texts and seniors for more information. However, this will provide you with a basic structure that your history should take.
