Commonly Used HIV Drugs in Pediatrics

Drug Dosage Route of Administration Indication for Use Precautions
Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF) 8 mg/kg once daily (max: 300 mg) Oral HIV-1 infection in pediatric patients 2 years of age and older Monitor renal function and bone health.
Emtricitabine (FTC) 3 mg/kg once daily (max: 200 mg) Oral HIV-1 infection in pediatric patients 2 years of age and older Monitor for adverse effects.
Dolutegravir (DTG) Weight-based dosing; consult pediatric guidelines Oral HIV-1 infection in pediatric patients 6 years of age and older Monitor for adverse effects and drug interactions.
Raltegravir (RAL) Weight-based dosing; consult pediatric guidelines Oral HIV-1 infection in pediatric patients 4 weeks of age and older Monitor for adverse effects and drug interactions.
Atazanavir (ATV) Weight-based dosing; consult pediatric guidelines Oral HIV-1 infection in pediatric patients 6 years of age and older Monitor for hyperbilirubinemia and drug interactions.
