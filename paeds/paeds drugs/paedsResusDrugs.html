Commonly Used Pediatric Resuscitation Drugs

Drug Pediatric Dosage Route of Administration Indication for Use
Adrenaline (Epinephrine) 0.01 mg/kg every 3-5 minutes Intravenous, intraosseous, or endotracheal Cardiac arrest, anaphylaxis, bronchospasm, hypotension
Adenosine 0.1 mg/kg rapid IV push, followed by a 1-2 mL saline flush Intravenous Supraventricular tachycardia
Aminophylline 5-6 mg/kg slow IV infusion over 20-30 minutes Intravenous Acute severe asthma
Bronchodilators (e.g., Salbutamol) 0.15-0.3 mg/kg per dose via nebulization or inhalation, up to 10 mg Nebulization or Inhalation Acute asthma exacerbation
Dextrose 10% 5-10 mL/kg IV bolus Intravenous Hypoglycemia
Diazepam (Valium) 0.2-0.5 mg/kg IV or rectal dose, repeat in 5-10 minutes if needed Intravenous or Rectal Seizures, status epilepticus
Dobutamine 2-20 μg/kg/minute continuous IV infusion Intravenous Low cardiac output, heart failure
Dopamine 2-20 μg/kg/minute continuous IV infusion Intravenous Low cardiac output, shock
Glycopyrrolate 0.005-0.01 mg/kg IV or IM, every 2-4 hours Intravenous or Intramuscular Bradycardia, anticholinesterase poisoning
Midazolam 0.05-0.2 mg/kg IV or IM, repeat as needed Intravenous or Intramuscular Sedation, seizures
Morphine 0.05-0.1 mg/kg IV, IM, or subcutaneous Intravenous, Intramuscular, or Subcutaneous Pain relief
Ondansetron 0.15 mg/kg IV or oral dose Intravenous or Oral Nausea and vomiting
Phenobarbital 15-20 mg/kg IV over 15-20 minutes, repeat as needed Intravenous Seizures
Vasopressin 0.0003-0.002 units/kg IV Intravenous Cardiac arrest
