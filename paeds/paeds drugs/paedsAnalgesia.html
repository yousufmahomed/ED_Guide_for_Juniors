Commonly Used Analgesics in Pediatrics

Analgesic Dosage Route of Administration Precautions
Acetaminophen (Paracetamol) 15 mg/kg every 4-6 hours Oral or rectal Avoid excessive use to prevent liver damage.
Aspirin 10-20 mg/kg every 4-6 hours Oral Avoid in children with viral infections due to risk of Reye's syndrome.
Ibuprofen 5-10 mg/kg every 6-8 hours Oral Administer with food to reduce stomach irritation.
Morphine 0.05-0.2 mg/kg every 4 hours Intravenous, intramuscular, or subcutaneous Monitor for respiratory depression and sedation.
Tramadol 1-2 mg/kg every 6-8 hours Oral Use with caution in children with seizures or respiratory disorders.
