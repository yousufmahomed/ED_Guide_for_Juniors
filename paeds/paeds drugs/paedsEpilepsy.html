Commonly Used Anti-Epileptic Medications in Pediatrics

Medication Dosage Route of Administration Precautions
Carbamazepine 10-20 mg/kg/day Oral Monitor for potential adverse effects on liver function.
Lamotrigine 1-15 mg/kg/day Oral Titrate dose slowly to reduce the risk of rash.
Levetiracetam 20-60 mg/kg/day Oral or intravenous May cause behavioral changes; monitor closely.
Phenytoin 5-10 mg/kg/day Oral or intravenous Monitor serum drug levels due to potential toxicity.
Sodium Valproate 15-60 mg/kg/day Oral Monitor for liver function, platelet count, and bleeding.
