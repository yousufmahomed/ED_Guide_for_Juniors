Paediatric Dosage Adjustment Guidelines

Adjusting medication dosages for pediatric patients requires careful consideration of factors such as age, weight, renal function, and hepatic function. The following guidelines, formulas, and tips can assist healthcare professionals in ensuring safe and effective medication dosing for children.

Factors to Consider

  • Age: Children's age significantly impacts medication metabolism. Younger children may require lower doses per kilogram of body weight.
  • Weight: Dosages are often calculated based on weight, with milligrams per kilogram (mg/kg) being a common unit of measurement.
  • Renal Function: Impaired renal function can affect drug clearance. Creatinine clearance is commonly used to estimate renal function.
  • Hepatic Function: Impaired liver function can influence drug metabolism. The Child-Pugh score is a tool to assess hepatic impairment.

Formulas for Dosage Adjustment

Several formulas can aid in adjusting medication dosages for pediatric patients:

  • Creatinine Clearance (CrCl): (140 - age) x weight (kg) / (72 x serum creatinine (mg/dL))
  • Child-Pugh Score: (1.0 x bilirubin) + (0.7 x INR) + (1.8 x ascites) + (3.0 x encephalopathy)

Tips and Tricks

  • Always verify dosages using reputable pediatric drug references or guidelines.
  • Use weight-based dosing for accurate medication administration.
  • For liquid medications, use the patient's weight in kilograms to calculate the appropriate volume to administer.
  • Consider rounding doses to practical and safe increments (e.g., using commercially available strengths).
  • Monitor patients closely for adverse effects and therapeutic response, especially during dosage adjustments.

Consultation with Experts

Adjusting pediatric medication dosages is a complex task. Always consult with pediatric pharmacists, clinical pharmacologists, or experienced colleagues when in doubt.
